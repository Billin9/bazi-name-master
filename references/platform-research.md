# Platform Research

Use online trend research to understand current naming aesthetics without copying internet language directly into names.

## Platforms

Prioritize recent sampling from:

- Douyin
- Xiaohongshu
- Zhihu

Add other sources only when they clearly help.

## What To Look For

Collect observations in these buckets:

- repeated high-frequency characters
- repeated name structures
- recurring aesthetic moods
- recurring imagery themes
- strong signals of overuse or sameness

## What Not To Do

- do not copy hot memes into names
- do not copy influencer wording directly
- do not pretend a short platform sample is a nationwide statistical fact
- do not let trendiness override BaZi and naming quality

## Transforming Trend Signals

Convert platform signals into imagery categories such as:

- clarity
- moonlight
- mountain mist
- brightness
- gentleness
- steadiness
- elegance
- vitality

Then map those categories to classical Chinese sources.

Examples:

- "clean girl" style should not appear verbatim in the name; extract traits such as clarity, softness, lucidity, restraint
- "healing vibe" should not appear verbatim; extract meanings such as calmness, warmth, tenderness, ease
- "atmosphere feeling" should not appear verbatim; extract mood, distance, subtlety, haze, grace

## Output Requirement

Trend research must produce two concrete artifacts:

1. `避用字库`
Characters, patterns, or styles that look overused or too homogeneous.

2. `意象池`
A curated pool of reusable imagery and mood directions that can be reconstructed through classical sources.

If online access fails, say the trend section is provisional instead of fabricating findings.
